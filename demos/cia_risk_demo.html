Risk Management Attack Scenarios

Click on a scenario to see how it affects the CIA triad in a risk management context

📊 Data Breach

Unauthorized access to sensitive risk assessment reports, exposing organizational vulnerabilities.

Example: Risk register leaked to competitors
Attacks Confidentiality

🔑 Leaked Credentials

Security system passwords or access credentials exposed through phishing or social engineering.

Example: Admin passwords found in unsecured documents
Attacks Confidentiality

🚪 Unauthorized Access

Employees accessing confidential risk reports or compliance data beyond their authorization level.

Example: Junior staff viewing executive risk briefings
Attacks Confidentiality

📝 Falsified Audit Logs

Manipulation of security audit trails to hide unauthorized activities or compliance violations.

Example: Deleting failed login attempts from logs
Attacks Integrity

📈 Manipulated Risk Assessment

Altering risk scores or assessment data to hide vulnerabilities or justify budget cuts.

Example: Changing risk rating from "High" to "Low"
Attacks Integrity

📋 Altered Compliance Reports

Modifying compliance documentation to falsely show adherence to ISO 27001 or other standards.

Example: Backdating security policy reviews
Attacks Integrity

💻 System Downtime

Critical security management systems become unavailable due to technical failures or attacks.

Example: SIEM system crash during incident
Attacks Availability

🔐 Ransomware Attack

Security systems encrypted by ransomware, preventing access to risk data and monitoring tools.

Example: Cannot access incident response playbooks
Attacks Availability

🌐 DDoS on Monitoring

Security monitoring and alerting systems overwhelmed, creating blind spots in threat detection.

Example: SOC dashboard becomes unresponsive
Attacks Availability

📊 Risk Management Perspective

In IT Security Management, the CIA triad forms the foundation for:

  • Risk Identification: What assets need CIA protection?
  • Risk Assessment: How likely are threats to each CIA pillar?
  • Control Selection: Which controls address specific CIA risks?
  • Monitoring: Are CIA properties being maintained?

ISO 27001 requires organizations to implement controls that protect all three pillars as part of their Information Security Management System (ISMS).
