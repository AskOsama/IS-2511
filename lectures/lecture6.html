1. Risk Treatment Revisited

What is Risk Treatment?

Risk treatment is a collective term for all the tactics, options, and strategies chosen to respond to a specific risk, to achieve the desired outcome concerning the threat.

Important Understanding

Risk treatment is not a concept functioning on its own. On the contrary, it should always be examined, understood, and implemented as part of a bigger whole, i.e., risk management.

2. Importance of Risk Treatment

Goals of Risk Treatment

The goal of risk treatment is to:

  • Remove risk entirely where possible
  • Reduce the impact or likelihood of risk
  • Redirect residual risk to other parties
  • Develop risk treatment plans as part of protecting the business

Why It Matters

Developing risk treatment plans is an integral part of protecting the business by making sure that risk does not take into action instead of dealing with it. Effective risk treatment relies on identifying the different types of risk against that particular business entity.

3. Five Steps of Risk Treatment

In the risk treatment process, it is recommended to follow five main steps to ensure the correct logistics and effectiveness of the strategy:

Five Steps of Risk Treatment Process

1

Brainstorming

Select the right risk treatment option

2

Planning

Plan and use the options chosen

3

Examining

Examine the effectiveness of chosen tactics

4

Deciding

Decide if residual risk is acceptable

5

Additional Treatment

Implement new activities if risk is not acceptable

4. Step 1: Brainstorming and Selecting the Right Option

Example: Cloud Migration Risk

Risk identified: Sensitive customer data could be exposed during cloud migration.

Brainstormed Options:

  • Avoid the risk by not moving sensitive data to the cloud
  • Reduce the risk by encrypting data and using secure access controls
  • Share the risk by using cyber-insurance
  • Accept the risk if impact is low

Chosen Option:

Reduce the risk (encryption, strong access controls, secure cloud provider)

5. Step 2: Planning and Use of Options Chosen

Create a Concrete Plan

A concrete plan is created with specific actions:

  • Select a certified cloud provider
  • Encrypt data before migration
  • Implement multi-factor authentication
  • Train employees on cloud security
  • Assign responsibilities and timelines

The controls are then implemented according to the plan.

6. Step 3: Examining the Effectiveness of Chosen Tactics

Verify Controls Are Working

The organization checks if controls are working by:

  • Performing penetration testing
  • Running vulnerability scans
  • Reviewing access logs
  • Conducting a security audit

Example Result

Some misconfigured storage detected and fixed during the security audit.

7. Step 4: Deciding Whether Residual Risk Is Acceptable

Evaluate Remaining Risk

After controls are implemented:

  • Likelihood of breach: Low
  • Impact: Medium
  • Management compares this with the company's risk appetite

Decision Point

If the residual risk is still higher than the organization's risk appetite, additional treatment activities must be implemented (proceed to Step 5).

8. Step 5: Implementing New Treatment Activities

When Residual Risk Is Not Acceptable

Additional actions are taken to further reduce the risk:

  • Add real-time monitoring
  • Apply stricter access policies
  • Schedule monthly security reviews
  • Improve incident response plan

Outcome

After implementing additional controls, the residual risk becomes acceptable and is formally approved by management.

9. Risk Treatment Plan and ISO 27001

ISO 27001 Certification

Risk treatment plans are an important part of any business's IT security strategy, and are especially critical for those seeking ISO 27001 certification.

What is a Risk Treatment Plan?

A risk treatment plan is a formal document that outlines the steps that will be taken to mitigate the risks associated with ISO 27001 certification. The risk treatment plan should be tailored to your organization's specific needs.

Why You Need One

  • It provides a roadmap for achieving compliance and certification
  • It is a required part of the ISO 27001 certification process
  • Organizations must outline the planned steps to mitigate IT security risks

10. Types of IT Risks Your Business Might Face

There are many risks that must be considered when seeking ISO 27001 certification. Some of the more common risks include:

Risk Type Description Mitigation Strategy
Data Breaches Devastating impact on reputation and finances Data encryption and access control
Malware Software causing extensive system damage Antivirus software and firewalls
Phishing Online scam tricking users into disclosing sensitive information Security awareness training for employees
Social Engineering Attacks relying on human interaction to obtain sensitive information Security awareness training for employees
Denial of Service Attacks rendering websites or services unavailable Intrusion detection and prevention systems
Data Loss Due to hardware failure, human error, or malicious attacks Strong backup and recovery measures
System Downtime Significant impact on business operations Contingency and recovery measures
Application Vulnerabilities Exploited by attackers to gain access to sensitive data Application whitelisting and sandboxing
Insider Threats Internal users accessing sensitive information and systems Least privilege principles and access control
External Threats From competitors, criminals, or nation-states Perimeter security and intrusion detection

Summary and Key Takeaways

What We've Learned

  1. Risk Treatment is a collective term for all tactics, options, and strategies chosen to respond to a specific risk. It is always part of the larger risk management process.
  2. The importance of risk treatment lies in removing, reducing, or redirecting residual risk to protect the business proactively.
  3. The five-step process includes: brainstorming, planning, examining effectiveness, deciding on residual risk acceptability, and implementing additional treatment if needed.
  4. A concrete example (cloud migration) demonstrates how the five steps are applied in practice, from identifying the risk through to formal approval of residual risk.
  5. Risk treatment plans are essential for ISO 27001 certification and provide a roadmap for mitigating IT security risks.
  6. Common IT risks include data breaches, malware, phishing, social engineering, denial of service, data loss, system downtime, application vulnerabilities, insider threats, and external threats.
